Where you are

Kijimadaira, northern Nagano

A small rural village in the mountains of northern Nagano. Quiet, authentic, and remarkably well-placed — multiple ski resorts within 20 minutes, onsens in every direction, snow monkeys 30 minutes away, and the historic iiyama Line running right through the valley.

⛷ Ski Resorts

11+ resorts within 60 minutes. Kijimadaira Snow Resort is 5 min away. Nozawa, Madarao, Shiga Kogen all close by.

5 min – 60 min drive → 🍜 Eating & Drinking

Lawson convenience store directly opposite. Local restaurants, izakaya, bakeries and cafés all in easy walking distance.

Walking distance → ♨ Onsens

From quiet local baths in Iiyama to the famous public baths of Nozawa Onsen village. Some private family options too.

5 min – 40 min drive → 🐒 Snow Monkeys

Jigokudani — the only place in the world where wild macaques bathe in natural hot springs. A short forest walk to reach them.

~35 min drive → 🥾 Hikes & Cycling

Mt. Kosha, Kayanotaira Kogen forest, the Shin-etsu Trail. Plus quiet country roads and Madarao mountain trails for bikes.

From the house → 🚃 The iiyama Line

One of Japan's great scenic train journeys — especially stunning during cherry blossom season. Connects to Nagano and the Shinkansen.

Hot Springs

Onsens of the area

Onsens are a normal part of daily life in rural Japan, especially in mountain regions like northern Nagano. They're used for relaxation and recovery after skiing or time outdoors. Options range from quiet local baths to the famous public bathhouses of Nozawa Onsen.

♨

What to expect

  • Nude, gender-separated bathing
  • Wash before entering the bath
  • Towels stay out of the water
  • Quiet, respectful behaviour
  • Some tattoo restrictions
  • ⭐ = private family options available

If you're new to onsens, the routine is easy to learn and quickly becomes a highlight of any stay in Japan.

Wildlife

Jigokudani Snow Monkey Park

The only place in the world where wild monkeys are known to bathe in natural hot springs. Located in the Yokoyu River valley at around 850m above sea level, on the edge of Shiga Kogen in northern Nagano.

Distance from house ~35 min Drive to car park
Walk to monkeys ~30 min Forest trail each way
Open Year-round Best in winter (Dec–Mar)

About the park

The park is just minutes from Shiga Kogen Ski Resort, making it easy to combine a ski day and a monkey visit. The area has long been home to troops of wild Japanese macaques who live here year-round in their natural habitat.

Since opening in 1964, Jigokudani has become internationally recognised for wildlife observation and photography. The short forest walk to reach the monkeys is especially memorable in winter, with snow on the trees and the sound of the river below.

The monkeys are most active and most visible in winter, when they cluster around the hot spring pool to keep warm. It's one of those things that looks exactly like the photos — and still manages to exceed expectations.

Combine with a visit to the nearby Shibu Onsen or Yudanaka Onsen for a full day out. The town of Yudanaka at the end of the Nagano Electric Railway line is well worth exploring.

Hikes & Cycling

Forested trails, mountain routes, and scenic backroads surround Kijimadaira. From gentle valley walks to alpine hikes and cycling through cedar forests, there are options for all levels within 20–40 minutes of the house.

Moderate · Year-round
Mt. Kosha

The iconic conical mountain overlooking Kijimadaira. Forested switchbacks lead to a 360° summit with panoramic views of the Northern Alps and Chikuma River. You can see the house from the top.

3.5–4.5 hrs round trip 1,351m peak Moderate difficulty
Easy · Family-friendly
Kayanotaira Kogen

The "Most Beautiful Forest in Japan." A high-altitude plateau of 300-year-old beeches at ~1,500m. Flat, well-maintained boardwalks — ideal for photography, forest bathing, and escaping summer heat.

1–2 hrs loop ~1,500m elevation Easy difficulty

Shin-etsu Trail

One of Japan's great long-distance trails, passing directly through the Kijimadaira area. Two sections are accessible as day hikes from the house.

Section 3 — The Samurai Pass
12.7 km 5–6 hours

Historic Tomikura Pass and views from Mt. Kuroiwa.

Section 4 — The Heart of the Trail
8.5 km 5–6 hours Steeper terrain

Mt. Nabekura and the "Valley of Giant Trees." Views of the Sea of Japan.

Cycling

Bike riding

The area offers relaxed countryside cycling and access to longer gravel and forest rides. Bikes can be hired from the shed at the house. Quiet village roads, open farmland, and mountain views make it an easy way to explore Kijimadaira.

  • Madarao Big Ten Trails — gravel, forest roads and single-track for mountain and gravel bikes

The iiyama Line

One of Japan's great scenic rail journeys — a single-track rural line through the mountains of northern Nagano. Especially spectacular during cherry blossom season in late April, when the tracks pass through a tunnel of pink blossoms along the Chikuma River valley.

Key Stops

  • Nagano (Shinkansen connection)
  • Yudanaka (Snow Monkey access)
  • Nakano
  • Iiyama ★ (closest to the house)
  • Kijimadaira ★
  • Tsunan
  • Tokamachi (Joetsu Line connection)

Useful Routes

  • Tokyo → House Shinkansen to Nagano (~2 hrs), then iiyama Line to Kijimadaira (~1.5 hrs)
  • Snow Monkeys day trip Iiyama Line to Yudanaka, then bus to Jigokudani park
  • Cherry blossom season Iiyama Line in late April — the line itself is the destination
  • Joetsu & coast Continue north from Kijimadaira toward Tokamachi and the Sea of Japan coast
