The House

Comfort meets
Japanese tradition

Kiji Yuki sits in the mountain village of Kijimadaira in Nagano Prefecture — quietly off the beaten track, while still within easy reach of world-class skiing, onsens, and everything that makes this part of Japan special.

Tatami floors, shoji screens, a deep soaking bath, and a heated kotatsu waiting for you after a day on the slopes.

With space for up to 14 guests it's made for a big family trip. The brand new drying and mud room is a game-changer — come back to warm, dry kit every morning. Not fancy, but set up for people who want to actually live in Japan for a week.

Year-Round

Any season is
a good season

Winter · Dec – Mar

Ski & Onsen

Multiple resorts minutes away with Japan's famous powder. Warm up at outdoor onsens under falling snow.

Spring · Apr – May

Cherry Blossoms

The valley turns pink. The iiyama Line in bloom is one of Japan's great train journeys.

Summer · Jun – Sep

Hikes & Cycling

Alpine trails, hire bikes from the shed. Snow monkey park open all year at Jigokudani.

Autumn · Oct – Nov

Foliage

Red and gold maples across the hillsides. Cool air, no crowds, excellent local restaurants.

Where You'll Be

Kijimadaira, Nagano

⛷ Ski Resorts

Nozawa Onsen, Shiga Kogen and more — Japan's best powder, far fewer crowds than Hakuba.

♨ Onsens

The Nozawa public baths are free and completely unforgettable. Several options nearby.

🐒 Snow Monkeys

Jigokudani Monkey Park is a short drive — wild macaques in hot springs, open all year.

🌸 Cherry Blossoms

The Chikuma River valley and iiyama Line in late April and early May are spectacular.

Getting Here

How to get there

By Train

01Tokyo → Iiyama: ~1 hr 45 min direct on the Hokuriku Shinkansen
02Iiyama → Kijimadaira: local bus or taxi, ~15 min
✦One of the easier bullet train journeys from Tokyo — no changes needed

By Hire Car

01Take the Shinkansen to Nagano city or Iiyama, then pick up a hire car
02Nagano city → Kijimadaira: ~1 hr · Iiyama → Kijimadaira: ~15 min
03Parking for 2 cars at the house
✦The best option for ski trips — carry your own gear and go at your own pace
