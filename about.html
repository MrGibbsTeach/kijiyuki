The House

Our shared family holiday home

Kiji Yuki is our shared family holiday home in Kijimadaira, a small rural town in northern Nagano. We use the house throughout the year with our families and occasionally open it to close friends and family so others can enjoy the area as we do.

"Staying here won't always be about being a tourist behind closed doors."

It's about living like the locals do, even for a short time.

In Winter

Embracing everyday realities

In winter, staying here also means embracing the everyday realities of mountain village life: clearing snow, managing wet gear, and adjusting plans to weather and conditions.

"For us, that's part of the experience — one of the things that makes staying here special rather than inconvenient."

A morning spent digging out the car, warm hands wrapped around a coffee, looking out at snow-covered rooftops — that's Kijimadaira. We wouldn't trade it.

The Community

Kijimadaira is a close-knit community

People here know each other, look out for one another, and take pride in their town. Because of that, we take our responsibility to the local community seriously — and we ask our guests to do the same.

This is new for us, and we're still learning how best to share the house while doing right by the people who live here all year round.

  • 一 Sort rubbish and recycling properly, following local rules
  • 二 Be mindful of noise, especially in the evenings
  • 三 Respect shared spaces, neighbours, and the village pace of life
What We Ask

We ask our guests to

  • 一 Treat the house with care, as you would your own family home
  • 二 Be considerate of neighbours and the wider community
  • 三 Engage with the place respectfully, not just pass through it
A Good Fit?

If this resonates with you

If that approach resonates with you, this house will be a great base for skiing, exploring, and experiencing Japan in a genuine way.

The skiing is world-class. The onsens are extraordinary. The snow monkey park is a short drive. And on a quiet Tuesday in February, with the village streets empty and snow falling past the window while the kotatsu heats the room — there's nowhere quite like it.

Practical Questions

Things people usually ask

  • ? Do I need a car? It's strongly recommended, especially in winter. You can reach Kijimadaira by train and taxi, but having a car makes getting to ski resorts and onsens much easier. Parking for two cars at the house.
  • ? Is it suitable for young children? Yes. The house is big, warm, and unfussy. The kotatsu lounge is perfect for small kids, and many of the nearby onsens have family-friendly facilities. The snow monkey park is a genuine hit with children of all ages.
  • ? Do I need to speak Japanese? Not at all. Most ski resorts and tourist spots have English signage. A translation app helps for restaurants and shops, but you'll get by comfortably without any Japanese.
  • ? What should I bring for skiing? Your own ski boots if you have them — rental boots are fine but your own are better. Everything else (skis, poles, helmets, clothing layers) can be hired at the resorts. The drying room means wet gear is sorted each evening.
  • ? How far are the ski resorts? There's a small local resort just 8 minutes by car — great for a quick morning run or a first day on the snow. Nozawa Onsen and Madarao are around 20–25 minutes. Shiga Kogen and the Myoko resorts are 50 minutes to an hour. All are far less crowded than Hakuba and have excellent snow quality.
  • ? What's the WiFi like? Coming soon — we're getting this sorted. In the meantime, we recommend picking up an eSIM before you travel.
  • ? What's the best time to visit? Winter (January–March) for skiing and powder snow. Late April for cherry blossoms along the iiyama Line. Summer for hiking and cycling with no crowds. Autumn for foliage and cool air.
